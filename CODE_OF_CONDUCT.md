# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We are committed to making participation in the weather-decoder project a
respectful and constructive experience for everyone, regardless of background,
identity, experience level, or viewpoint.

Everyone who participates in this project is expected to help create an
environment where people can contribute, ask questions, review work, and
discuss technical decisions without harassment or intimidation.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that supports a healthy project community include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, experiences, and skill levels.
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback in good faith.
- Keeping discussions focused on the work and the needs of the project.
- Acknowledging mistakes and correcting them when appropriate.
- Showing patience with new contributors and people unfamiliar with the codebase.

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Insults, personal attacks, or demeaning comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Discriminatory jokes, slurs, or exclusionary comments.
- Repeatedly pushing a discussion after maintainers have asked for it to stop.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make another participant feel unsafe or
  unwelcome.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull
requests, discussions, code review, release work, and any other public or
private communication connected to this project.

It also applies when someone represents the project in public spaces, such as
using an official project account, presenting project work, or acting as a
project maintainer.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it to the project maintainers. Use private repository channels
where available. If no private channel is available, contact a maintainer
directly or open an issue with minimal public detail and ask for a private
follow-up.

Reports should include:

- A description of what happened.
- Where and when it happened.
- The people involved, if known.
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or context.

Maintainers will handle reports with discretion and will avoid sharing details
beyond what is needed to review and respond to the issue.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying expectations and responding
to Code of Conduct violations. They may take any action they consider
appropriate and proportionate, including:

- Asking a participant to stop harmful behavior.
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, issues, or pull requests.
- Temporarily or permanently limiting participation in project spaces.
- Reporting behavior to the relevant platform or authority when necessary.

Participants who are asked to stop unacceptable behavior are expected to comply
immediately.

## Maintainer standards

Maintainers are expected to apply this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly.
Maintainers who do not follow or enforce it in good faith may be removed from
their maintainer role by the project owner or other project leadership.

## Attribution
